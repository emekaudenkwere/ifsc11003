Spirit (MER-A)

Mission goals & overview: Land as part of the Mars Exploration Rover (MER) program to search for evidence of past water and study rocks and soils.

Launch & landing: Launched June 10, 2003. Landed January 4, 2004 in Gusev Crater via airbag-assisted landing.

Major discoveries: Found strong evidence of past water-altered minerals and diverse rock types suggesting a watery past. Long operational life exceeding planned 90 sols.

Curiosity (MSL)

Mission goals & overview: Determine habitability â€” study Mars' climate and geology, and assess whether Gale Crater could have supported microbial life.

Launch & landing: Launched November 26, 2011. Landed August 6, 2012 in Gale Crater using sky crane.

Major discoveries: Evidence of ancient lakebeds, organic molecules, seasonal methane variations, diverse mineralogy including clay and silica deposits.

Key technical features: Nuclear power (RTG), ChemCam laser spectrometer, sample analysis labs (SAM, CheMin), complex robotic arm.

Perseverance (Mars 2020)

Mission goals & overview: Search for signs of ancient microbial life, collect and cache samples for future return, and test technologies for future human exploration.

Launch & landing: Launched July 30, 2020. Landed February 18, 2021 in Jezero Crater using sky crane and precision landing tech.

Major discoveries: Collected first sample caches, discovered diverse sedimentary rocks consistent with former river/lake environments, and produced oxygen from the Martian atmosphere using MOXIE.

Key technical features: Advanced sample caching system, SHERLOC and PIXL instruments, SuperCam, helicopter Ingenuity companion drone.
