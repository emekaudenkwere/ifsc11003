Frequently Asked Questions

Why does NASA send rovers to Mars?

To study the planet's geology, climate, and potential for life, and prepare for future human missions.

How do rovers land on Mars?

Using complex entry, descent, and landing systems including parachutes, retro rockets, and sky cranes.

What powers the rovers?

Curiosity and Perseverance use radioisotope thermoelectric generators (RTGs) for long-term energy.

Can rovers be controlled in real-time?

No. Due to the distance, commands are sent in advance and rovers operate semi-autonomously.
